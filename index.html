Meet Our Teams

Dr. Ahmed Abdulkareem

Dr. Ahmed Abdulkareem is a community development enthusiast who loves to impact positively the lives of people and give back to society. He attended college at Ilorin Comprehensive High School and subsequently went to the better by far University of Ilorin in Nigeria to study Medicine, where he graduated in 2015. Ahmed was the overall best student throughout his six years in secondary school and got a certificate of commendation as an Academic Ambassador from the same school. In medical school, he served as the Read More Class representative, President of both the Quiz and Research & Journals Clubs, and Chairman of Talent Hunt and Academic Committees for Ilumsa and Muslim Students Society, respectively. He was the President of the Medical and Health CDS group while serving in Kalgo LGA, Kebbi state. As a medical doctor, Ahmed has the vision to provide high-quality care and to observe international standards, and this has informed his decision to obtain certifications from both the United States and United kingdom Medical councils. He currently works for the NHS in England, United Kingdom, and has a special interest in Emergency Medicine, Medical Leadership, Education, and Research. Being an aspiring leader Dr. Abdulkareem has attended courses on communication, leadership, and success. These include; ‘Effective Communication for Healthy Outcome’ and ‘Developing, Mentoring and Supporting Youth Leadership’ both through the Young African Leaders Initiative (YALI), ‘Improving Communication Skills’, ‘Success’, and ‘Leading the Life You Want’, all from the University of Pennsylvania. He also has diploma certifications in Leadership and Management in Health and Fundamental of Global Health Research, both from the University of Washington. Dr. Abdulkareem is a Fellow of the Institute of Management Consultants (IMC-Nigeria), a Certified Management Consultant by the IMC-Nigeria, and a Certified Management Specialist from the London Graduate School. He is the Founder and Chairman of the Board of Trustees of Champions Educational Foundation, an NGO based in Nigeria. Ahmedcine, as he’s fondly called, is a multiple intellectual awards winner, including Merit Awards, Certificates of recognition/ outstanding performances, Gold medals, and other medals from many regional/national competitions and other endeavors. He’s got inherent Leadership, Public speaking, Teaching, and Creative writing skills. His hobbies include Traveling, Mentoring, Motivational speaking, and Meditating. Ahmed is happily married with two beautiful daughters. Read Less

Ilias Kazeem

Ilias is a PhD Student and Teaching Fellow, at the School of Law, University of Aberdeen, United Kingdom. His research has significant impacts on SDG 16.7 dealing with "responsive, inclusive, participatory and representative decision-making at all levels", as his PhD thesis seeks to establish public participation in international investment arbitration of energy and natural resources (ENR) disputes, particularly for the protection of host communities of ENR projects. His wider research and professional activities also significantly impact SDG 7 on affordable and clean energy. He is a Barrister and Solicitor of Read More the Supreme Court of Nigeria, and he is an Associate of the Chartered Institute of Arbitrators UK. Ilias holds an LLM in energy and environmental law (with a Distinction) from the University of Aberdeen and an LLB with a First Class from the University of Ilorin, Nigeria. He is a member of the Energy Disputes Arbitration Centre (EDAC) Young Members Group. He is also a co-founder of Sholias LP in Lagos, Nigeria. Read Less

Seun Awogbenle

Seun Awogbenle is a Nigerian Author, policy, and communications professional. He is a member of the Technical Working Group for the preparation of the Medium-Term National Development Plan (MTNDP) 2021-2025 and Nigeria Agenda 2050 which is the official development framework for Nigeria. He contributes Op-ed on The Guardian Newspaper, The Cable and most recently ThisDay Newspaper. He is a regular feature on Channels TV, TV Continental TVC and Arise TV. He has also been previously Read More featured on NewsDay, a BBC World Service London, News and Current Affairs Programme, following his controversial Op-ed that called for an end to death penalty sentence in Nigeria. He is the Author of The Urgency of Now; Why Nigeria Needs a Vision of Prosperity, Protection and Liberty. The book which was foreword by The Founder/CEO of Cable Newspaper, Simon Kolawole, has 10 chapters that harped on the imperative of visioning, leadership, youth participation and active citizenship. He has spoken at different platforms including the TedX. and notably at the Lagos Youth Symposium, where he was acknowledged for his contribution by the Governor of Lagos State Mr Babajide Sanwo-Olu. He is an Associate Member of the Nigerian Institute of Public Relations NIPR. He was previously involved in shaping the strategic communication of Nigeria’s leading Electricity Distribution Company, Eko Electricity Distribution Company (EKEDC). Read Less
